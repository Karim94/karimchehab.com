ParkNosis

​Parkinson's Disease Symptoms Detection Mobile App​
November 2015 - May 2016
  • Researched, designed and developed a Native Android Mobile App for the early detection of Parkinson's Disease (PD) symptoms
  • Implemented 3 quantitative tests which collect physical data from patients using simple touchscreen and sensor tests on an Android Smartphone and a Samsung S2 Gear Smartwatch
  • Applied algorithms to analyze data and provide patients with results 
  • Used Parse Online Database for storage and retrieval of patient test data
  • Used GitHub to coordinate Version Control among the development team

Project Achievments:

  • Recieved exemplary feedback from the Department of Computer Science and Engineering and collegues at AUS
  • Participated at the Mobile Application Contest (MAC) 2016 held at Khalifa University in Abu Dhabi, UAE
  • Won 2nd place in the 11th IEEE Student Day 2016 competition held at Al-Ain University of Science & Technology in Abu Dhabi, UAE
  • Published a research paper titled "ParkNosis: Diagnosing Parkinson's Disease Using Mobile Phones" in the IEEE Healthcom 2016 - 18th International Conference on e-Health Networking, Applications and Services on September 17, 2016 in Germany
